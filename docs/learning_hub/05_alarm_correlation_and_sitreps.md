# Alarm Correlation and SITREPs

**Audience:** NOC engineers and shift leads
**Time to read:** 10 minutes

---

## How pedk.ai Correlates Alarms Across Domains

Traditional NOC alarm management presents every alarm in the order it arrives. A single transport link failure can produce hundreds of downstream alarms across multiple cells and services — each one a separate item in the queue. The NOC engineer's job becomes an archaeological dig through an alarm avalanche to find the one cause under the pile.

pedk.ai approaches this differently. It ingests alarms from all three network domains — Radio Access (RAN), Transport, and Core — and applies two correlation lenses simultaneously:

**Temporal correlation**: Alarms that arrive within a short window of each other are candidates for clustering. If 47 cell alarms fire within 90 seconds, they likely share a cause.

**Topology correlation**: Using the Dark Graph, pedk.ai checks whether the alarming entities are connected. A transport node failure upstream of 20 base stations explains why all 20 raise alarms at the same time.

The result is a **cluster** — a group of related alarms with a proposed root cause entity identified. A cluster of 47 alarms becomes one managed incident with a root cause hypothesis. Your queue shrinks from dozens of items to a handful of clusters.

Noise reduction from clustering is typically 40–80% of raw alarm volume on a busy network. The exact figure depends on network architecture and alarm density.

## Reading a SITREP

A SITREP (Situation Report) is pedk.ai's structured analysis of an incident cluster. It is generated after alarms are correlated and root cause analysis is complete.

**SITREP structure:**

```
[AI WATERMARK] This content was generated by pedk.ai AI.
Advisory only. Requires human review before action.

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
[Plain-language description of the fault and impact]

CONFIDENCE SCORE: 0.82

ROOT CAUSE
Entity:     [Network element ID and name]
Domain:     [RAN / Transport / Core]
Evidence:   [List of alarms and KPI deviations that support this conclusion]

AFFECTED ENTITIES
[List of downstream entities impacted]

SUBSCRIBER IMPACT
Estimated affected subscribers: [N]
Revenue at risk: [£N/hr] (estimate — flagged is_estimate: true)

RECOMMENDED ACTION
[Specific remediation steps]

CAUSAL CHAIN
[The topology path from root cause to affected services]
```

**How to read the confidence score:**

The score is not generated by the language model — it is calculated independently from Decision Memory similarity and expert causal template matching. A score of 0.82 means pedk.ai has found strong historical precedent for this pattern and the evidence matches a known causal template. A score of 0.55 means the pattern is unusual and the recommendation is based on weak evidence.

All subscriber and revenue impact figures labelled `is_estimate: true` are approximations. Do not use these numbers in regulatory reporting without independent verification.

## The Escalation Workflow: SitrepRouter Tiers

When a SITREP is generated, pedk.ai's SitrepRouter assigns it to the appropriate team tier and sets an escalation timer. If the incident is not resolved within the threshold, the system flags it for escalation to the next tier.

| Tier | Role | Typical responsibility |
|------|------|----------------------|
| **NOC Tier 1** | NOC Engineer | First responder — initial diagnosis and remote remediation |
| **NOC Tier 2** | NOC Manager | Escalation lead — non-standard remediation, vendor contact authorisation |
| **Field Operations** | Field Team | Physical site investigation — hardware, fibre, power |
| **Executive** | On-call Executive | Major outages affecting SLA obligations or regulatory reporting thresholds |

**Escalation timing by domain:**

| Domain | Severity | Initial tier | Escalate after | Escalate to |
|--------|----------|-------------|---------------|-------------|
| RAN | Critical | NOC Tier 1 | 15 min | NOC Manager |
| RAN | High | NOC Tier 1 | 30 min | NOC Manager |
| Transport | Critical | NOC Tier 1 | 15 min | Field Team |
| Transport | High | NOC Tier 1 | 30 min | Field Team |
| Core | Critical | NOC Manager | 10 min | Executive |
| Core | High | NOC Tier 1 | 20 min | NOC Manager |
| Any | Low | NOC Tier 1 | 120 min | — |

Transport faults at High severity and above have the `requires_field` flag set automatically — a field dispatch ticket should be pre-raised in parallel with remote diagnosis.

Core Critical incidents bypass Tier 1 entirely and go directly to the NOC Manager, reflecting the commercial and regulatory sensitivity of core network outages.

## How to Provide Feedback on SITREP Accuracy

Every SITREP has a feedback panel at the bottom of the incident detail view. Feedback is structured — it asks you to rate four specific dimensions rather than just thumbs up or down:

| Dimension | Question |
|-----------|---------|
| **Accuracy** | Was the root cause correct? |
| **Relevance** | Was the SITREP focused on the right entities? |
| **Actionability** | Were the recommended steps useful and executable? |
| **Timeliness** | Did the SITREP arrive in time to be useful? |

Each dimension is rated 1–5. You can also add a free-text note — particularly useful for overrides where you want to record what the correct root cause actually was.

The structured rating is significantly more useful to the system than the free text alone. A 1-star accuracy rating with the note "root cause wrong — the fault was the aggregation switch at site X, not the baseband unit at cell Y" tells the system exactly what to learn. "Not helpful" tells it nothing useful.

Your feedback target: rate >70% of SITREPs on accuracy, relevance, and actionability. This is tracked at team level and reviewed monthly by the NOC Manager.

## Multi-Domain Correlation Examples

**Example 1: Transport degradation causing sleeping cells**

A fibre link connecting an aggregation node to 14 base stations experiences intermittent packet loss. The loss is below the threshold for a hard alarm on the transport node. But on the RAN side, all 14 cells show rising `handover_success_rate` drops (devices leaving the cells), falling `throughput_mbps`, and reduced `PR_RRC_ConnEstabSucc_Sum`. None of the cells raise a sleeping cell alarm individually, because no single metric crosses its threshold.

pedk.ai clusters the 14 cells using topology correlation (they all share the same aggregation node in the Dark Graph) and temporal correlation (the degradation started at approximately the same time). It identifies the aggregation node as the root cause entity, with the transport domain as the fault domain. The SITREP recommends checking the fibre link and sets `requires_field: true`.

**Example 2: Core congestion appearing as RAN fault**

A core network gateway enters a congestion state. Calls begin dropping. RAN cells across a wide area generate radio link failure alarms. A naive view suggests a RAN problem. pedk.ai observes that the geographic spread of the affected cells is too wide and too uniform to be a localised RAN fault — no single RAN entity could affect cells across three regions simultaneously. The topology traversal finds the shared core gateway and identifies it as the probable root cause. The SITREP routes to the NOC Manager (Core domain, High severity) rather than dispatching field teams to individual cell sites.

In both cases, the cross-domain perspective is the key. Domain-isolated analysis would either miss the pattern entirely or send the team to the wrong layer of the network.
