# Getting Started with pedk.ai

**Audience:** New NOC engineers and shift leads
**Time to read:** 10 minutes

---

## What is pedk.ai?

pedk.ai is an AI-powered decision-support platform for Network Operations Centres (NOCs) at telecom operators. It ingests real-time telemetry from your radio access, transport, and core network elements, correlates alarms, detects degraded conditions that produce no alarms (sleeping cells), and generates Situation Reports (SITREPs) to help your team resolve incidents faster and with better context.

pedk.ai is not an autonomous system. At the default deployment level, it analyses, recommends, and explains — your team decides and acts. The platform is designed around a simple principle: the engineer who approves an action is responsible for that action, and pedk.ai exists to make that decision better-informed, not to remove it.

---

## Who is pedk.ai for?

- **NOC engineers** — your primary interface is the NOC dashboard. You will review AI-generated SITREPs, validate sleeping cell alerts, provide feedback on recommendations, and exercise overrides where the AI is wrong.
- **Shift leads** — you approve SITREPs at Human Gate 1 before actions are taken, manage escalation routing, and maintain oversight of the team's override rate.
- **NOC managers** — you close incidents at Human Gate 3, review team performance metrics, and monitor the platform's calibration quality.

## The 5 Deployment Modes

pedk.ai operates across five deployment stages. Your operator's platform team controls which stage is active. The stage determines what pedk.ai shows you and what it can do.

| Stage | What pedk.ai does | What you do |
|-------|------------------|-------------|
| **Offline PoC** | Analyses historical data only; no live ingestion | Evaluate accuracy against known past incidents |
| **Shadow Mode** | Runs live but all outputs are internal only; nothing is shown to operators | Work as normal; pedk.ai silently builds its baseline |
| **Advisory (Level 0)** | Generates SITREPs and recommendations; you see everything | Review AI analysis, decide whether to act, provide feedback |
| **Assisted (Level 1)** | Drafts ITSM tickets pre-populated from SITREP analysis | Review and approve every draft before dispatch |
| **Supervised / Gated Autonomous (Levels 2–3)** | Can execute certain pre-approved routine actions | Monitor continuously; veto within the override window |

Most operators begin production operations at Level 0 (Advisory). This is the recommended starting point and the configuration covered by the majority of this documentation.

Shadow Mode is a mandatory 30-day calibration period before Advisory is activated. During Shadow Mode, pedk.ai is invisible to NOC operators — you work as normal and pedk.ai silently compares its analysis against your actual decisions.

## Navigating the NOC Dashboard

The NOC dashboard is your main working environment. The key pages are:

| Page | URL | Purpose |
|------|-----|---------|
| Incidents | `/incidents` | Active incident list with AI cluster summaries and SITREP status |
| Sleeping Cells | `/sleeping-cells` | Cells flagged as potentially sleeping, with decay scores |
| Topology | `/topology` | Dark Graph visualisation — network topology with inferred dependencies |
| Divergence | `/divergence` | CMDB divergence report — where declared topology differs from observed |
| Reports | `/reports` | Shift reports, performance trends, SITREP history |
| Feedback | `/feedback` | Your feedback history and team accuracy metrics |
| Settings | `/settings` | Personal preferences, notification settings, RBAC scope overview |

The top navigation bar shows your current autonomy level and any active system alerts. A banner reading "Shadow Mode Active" means pedk.ai is running but none of its analysis is visible yet.

## Reading a SITREP

A SITREP (Situation Report) is pedk.ai's analysis of an active incident cluster. Every SITREP includes:

- **Executive summary** — plain-language description of the fault and affected entities
- **Root cause entity** — the network element pedk.ai has identified as the likely origin
- **Confidence score** — a number from 0.0 to 0.95. This is pedk.ai's self-assessed reliability for this specific recommendation
- **Evidence chain** — the alarms, KPI deviations, and topology relationships that led to this conclusion
- **Recommended action** — what pedk.ai suggests you do

All AI-generated content carries the watermark: *"This content was generated by pedk.ai AI. It is advisory only and requires human review before action."*

**Reading the confidence score:**

| Score range | What it means |
|-------------|---------------|
| 0.75 – 0.95 | High confidence — reliable, but review is still required |
| 0.50 – 0.74 | Moderate confidence — cross-check against raw alarms before acting |
| Below 0.50 | Low confidence — manual triage required; pedk.ai has insufficient evidence |

A score below 0.70 triggers a `[LOW CONFIDENCE]` flag on the SITREP. This is not a failure — it is the system telling you it needs your expertise.

## First Steps Checklist for a New NOC Engineer

Before your first shift with pedk.ai active, confirm the following:

- [ ] You have completed the initial 3-day pedk.ai training programme (AI fundamentals, human gate operations, feedback and override mechanics)
- [ ] Your user account is active and you can log in to the NOC dashboard
- [ ] You have reviewed your RBAC scope in `/settings` — confirm which human gates you are authorised to action
- [ ] You can navigate to `/incidents`, `/sleeping-cells`, and `/feedback` without errors
- [ ] You understand the override button: when the AI is wrong, you use it — and you add a specific reason code, not a vague note
- [ ] You know the degraded-mode procedure: if the backend is unavailable, continue using your existing OSS tools (Ericsson ENM / Nokia NetAct) and standard runbooks without waiting for pedk.ai to recover
- [ ] You have read the escalation matrix and know the P1 response SLA (15 minutes)
- [ ] You know that emergency service entities — those tagged `EMERGENCY_SERVICE` in the topology — are always automatically escalated to P1 and cannot be downgraded

If any item is incomplete, speak to your shift lead before beginning work.

## A Note on Your Role

Working with pedk.ai requires a different kind of attention than traditional alarm triage. The question shifts from "what is happening?" — which pedk.ai answers first — to "is the AI correct, and what should I do about it?"

This is a supervisory role. The engineers who get the most from pedk.ai are those who engage critically with every SITREP, exercise overrides freely when warranted, and treat feedback as a contribution to a system that improves through use. The engineers who get the least from it are those who treat AI recommendations as something to approve without review.

Both behaviours show up in the audit trail. Override rate is tracked — both too low (rubber-stamping) and too high (AI not helping) are signals that something needs attention.
